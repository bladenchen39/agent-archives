## 4. Trump-Xi Summit — Beijing (May 14-15)

President Trump met President Xi in Beijing for a two-day summit. Analysis described it as a **"stalemate summit"** with few concrete breakthroughs.

### What Was Agreed
- **Boeing jet deal:** China agreed to purchase 200 Boeing jets (potential to grow to 750)
- **Board of Trade established** to oversee tariff reduction on ~$30 billion in goods
- Both leaders expressed shared interest in avoiding nuclear weapons in Iran

### What Was Not Resolved
- No commitment from China to support U.S.-led efforts on Iran sanctions enforcement
- No breakthrough on critical minerals supply (which China has restricted)
- No resolution on sanctions against Chinese companies purchasing Iranian oil
- Trump noted he's "considering" removing some sanctions on Chinese firms

## 5. Oil & Strait of Hormuz

**Brent Crude:** $109.26/bbl (close, May 15) — up ~$8 from last week

### Situation
- Strait of Hormuz remains **effectively restricted** since February
- Iran proposing a new "designated route management system" — offering preferential passage to cooperating nations for a fee
- Some East Asian ships (Chinese) beginning to pass through under new protocols
- 38-nation multinational military mission (co-chaired by UK, France) organizing to support future freedom of navigation
- Diplomatic talks remain stalled; Iran demanding sovereignty recognition

### Economic Impact
- Oil up ~$8 in a single week reflects escalating supply fears
- Energy costs now the primary driver of headline inflation
- Gasoline station sales up 2.8% MoM in April (price-driven)

## Analysis: What This All Means

**Markets at an inflection point.** The mid-week record highs followed by the Friday sell-off captures the tension perfectly. Optimism about AI, earnings, and diplomacy is clashing with the reality of $109 oil, sticky 3.8% inflation, and a new Fed chair whose disposition is unknown.

**The Warsh era starts under pressure.** He takes over with inflation above target, oil spiking, and growth slowing. His first decision — at the June FOMC — will set the tone. A rate hike would shock markets; a hold signals continuity.

**The Strait of Hormuz is the dominant variable.** Oil gained ~$8 this week alone. At $109, the economic drag is material. Every week the strait stays disrupted pushes inflation higher and growth lower. The Iran transit fee proposal adds a new wrinkle — effectively institutionalizing the disruption.

**The summit was a non-event for markets.** The Boeing deal is nice for one company, but no structural trade deal means the tariffs and sanctions dance continues. The lack of China cooperation on Iran enforcement is a meaningful negative.

**Consumer is holding up but shifting.** Retail sales show spending is rotating from goods to energy-necessities. The department store decline (-3.2%) vs gas station rise (+2.8%) tells the story. This is sustainable only as long as the labor market holds.
