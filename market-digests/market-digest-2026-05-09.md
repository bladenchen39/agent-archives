## 6. Financial Stability — Private Credit Warning

The **Financial Stability Board (FSB)** published a report on **May 6** flagging systemic vulnerabilities in the private credit market.

### Key Concerns
- **Market size:** $1.5–$2.0 trillion in assets (end of 2024)
- **Valuation opacity:** Lack of public ratings and transparent pricing
- **Borrower quality:** Higher leverage and lower credit quality vs. public markets
- **Interconnectedness:** Deep links with banks, insurers, and private equity firms
- **Liquidity mismatch:** Funds offering redemption options hold illiquid assets
- **Concentration:** Heavy exposure to tech, health care, and services sectors

**Why It Matters:** This sector has grown rapidly outside traditional regulatory oversight. A prolonged downturn could expose hidden leverage and create contagion through bank/insurer interconnections.

## Analysis: What This All Means

**Markets & Inflation:** The simultaneous strength in equities (record highs) and oil ($100+) is an unusual combination. It signals investors are betting on economic resilience despite a supply shock in energy markets. The Fed transition adds a layer of uncertainty — a Warsh-led Fed may be quicker to raise rates or slower to cut them if inflation reignites.

**Geopolitical Risk:** The Strait of Hormuz closure is the single biggest wild card. Every week it remains disrupted, the risk of a sustained inflationary shock grows. The ceasefire's fragility means this is far from resolved.

**Financial Stability:** The FSB's private credit warning is significant because this market has grown into systemic territory without corresponding oversight. If 2023 taught us anything (Silicon Valley Bank, Credit Suisse), it's that hidden leverage in opaque markets can crystallize quickly.

**Labor Market:** The jobs data supports a "soft landing" narrative — the economy is adding jobs, but not so fast that it reignites wage-driven inflation. This keeps the Fed on hold, which markets broadly welcome.
